Priority 2 — Fix the leak

Sales Pipeline

Leads are going cold after first contact. This is a follow-up and nurture problem — not a lead quality problem. Here's the system to fix it.

Why leads go cold: You respond, they're interested, life gets in the way, they forget. Without a structured follow-up, you're relying on them to re-engage. A 5-touch follow-up sequence increases close rates by 60–80%. Most deals close on touch 4–7.
Stage 1
First contact
  • Respond within 1 hour (use AI template)
  • Ask 3 qualifying questions
  • Add to CRM / Google Sheet immediately
  • Send property pack or rental brochure
Stage 2
Follow-up #1 (Day 2)
  • Check in — did you receive the info?
  • Add value: market insight or property highlight
  • Propose a call or WhatsApp chat
Stage 3
Follow-up #2 (Day 5)
  • Trigger: urgency or scarcity angle
  • "This property had 3 enquiries this week"
  • Send a relevant testimonial or case study
Stage 4
Follow-up #3 (Day 10)
  • Change the medium (call if email, email if WhatsApp)
  • Ask: what's holding you back?
  • Offer a virtual tour or concierge call
Stage 5
Long nurture (Monthly)
  • Add to Mailchimp newsletter
  • Monthly market update email
  • New property announcements
  • Re-engage with seasonal content

Building the Moat

A moat is what makes Roam hard to copy. Without one, any well-funded competitor can replicate you. These are the 5 moat-building moves — build them in parallel as revenue grows.

Your natural moat starting point: Roam sits at the intersection of travel, rentals, and property — most companies do one of these. The full ecosystem (rent → love a city → buy in it → invest through Roam) is your moat. No Airbnb, no traditional agent does all three. Own that narrative.
Moat 1
The Curated Network
Every property Roam lists should be vetted against a quality standard. Not just "available" but "Roam-approved." This curation takes time and expertise to replicate — and builds trust with both sides of the marketplace. Create a "Roam Standard" checklist for properties.
🔑 Start: Define the Roam quality criteria
How to define the Roam Property Standard
⏱ 1 hour
1
Create a "Roam Standard" checklist in a Google Doc
Every property you list — owned or partner — must pass this checklist before it goes live. This is your quality moat. Start with these non-negotiables:
ROAM PROPERTY STANDARD — CHECKLIST MUST HAVE (no exceptions): ☐ Professional photography (min 15 photos) ☐ Confirmed WiFi speed (min 50mbps for remote workers) ☐ Verified owner/manager contact available within 2 hours ☐ Accurate listing description reviewed by Roam team ☐ Clear pricing with no hidden fees ☐ Guest review or reference available ☐ Emergency contact process in place STRONGLY PREFERRED: ☐ Concierge or welcome service ☐ Welcome pack / local guide ☐ Flexible check-in/check-out (within reason) ☐ Weekly cleaning included for stays 7+ days
2
Apply this retrospectively to your current 80+ properties
Add a "Roam Standard" column to your Property Audit sheet. Mark each property: Pass ✅ / Needs Work ⚠️ / Fail ❌. Any that fail stay unlisted until the issue is resolved.
This will feel uncomfortable because some properties may not pass. That's the point — one bad guest experience undoes 10 great ones. Quality control is your moat.
3
Add "Roam Verified" to every passing property on the website
A small badge on each listing: "Roam Verified ✦". This signals to guests that the property has been checked — and to competitors that you have a standard they'd have to replicate.
Moat 2
Member Data & Preference Engine
As you build members, you learn exactly what premium travellers want — preferred cities, property types, budgets, travel patterns. Over time, this data powers better recommendations than any competitor without that history. Your CRM is the seed of this.
🔑 Start: Capture preferences from day one
Moat 3
The Travel → Buy Pipeline
Roam is the only platform where someone can rent a property in Dubai for a month, fall in love with it, and buy through the same company. This conversion pathway is uniquely valuable — and produces high-margin property sales from existing happy customers.
🔑 Start: Tag renters who visit multiple times
Moat 4
Partner & Supply Lock-in
Exclusive or preferred relationships with property owners, concierge partners, and travel agents create switching costs on the supply side. If your best property owners are loyal to Roam because you generate consistent bookings, competitors can't easily poach them.
🔑 Start: Partner agreement template
Moat 5
Brand & Community
The hardest moat to copy and the most valuable. When "Roam" means something to a specific type of traveller — curious, premium, global — the brand becomes its own acquisition engine. A private community (Slack, Circle, WhatsApp) turns members into advocates.

1
Understand the system — read this first
5 minutes · No tools needed
⌄

Here's what we're building: a system that finds luxury travel agents who book clients into Bali, Cape Town, London, Dubai and Ibiza — and reaches out on your behalf until they're ready to partner with Roam.

The 3 tools you'll use
Apollo.io — finds travel agent names, companies and email addresses. Free.

Instantly.ai — sends emails from your Roam address, handles follow-ups, detects replies. ~$37/month.

Make.com + Claude — when someone replies, AI reads it, drafts a response, and only pings you if they're warm. Free tier covers this.
Why not LinkedIn? LinkedIn bans automated messaging and will restrict your account. Email is safer, more scalable, and travel agents check email far more than DMs.
Realistic expectations: From 100 agents, expect 30–40% open rate, 5–10% positive replies, and 2–5 active booking partners. That's 10–20 bookings in the first 60 days.

Build the AI reply draft flow
45 minutes · The main build
⌄
Build this 3-module flow in Make
Module 1: Gmail — Watch Emails
Add Gmail module → "Watch Emails" → connect Roam Gmail → folder: Inbox · filter: subject contains "Re:"

Module 2: Claude AI — Generate Response
Add HTTP module → URL: https://api.anthropic.com/v1/messages · Method: POST
Header: x-api-key = your Claude API key
Prompt: "You are an assistant for Roam. A travel agent has replied to our outreach. Read their reply and: 1) Classify as WARM or COLD. 2) If WARM, draft a short friendly reply that moves toward a call. If COLD, output 'COLD — no response needed'. Reply: {email body}"

Module 3: Gmail — Create Draft
Add Gmail → "Create a Draft" → To: sender email · Subject: original subject · Body: AI output
Getting your Claude API key: Go to console.anthropic.com → API Keys → Create Key. Free tier covers hundreds of email responses.
Feels technical? Paste the instructions above into Claude and say "walk me through building this Make.com scenario step by step." It will guide you through the whole thing.
